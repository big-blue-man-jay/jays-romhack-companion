Mechanics Guide

This guide covers all custom mechanics, rule changes, and new features introduced in this romhack. If something feels different from a standard HeartGold playthrough, you will find the explanation here. This page is updated as new mechanics are added.

Battle
No EVs Battle

Effort Values have been removed from the game entirely. Pokemon do not gain EVs from battles and no EV-boosting items have any effect. All stat calculations are based purely on base stats, IVs, nature, and level — keeping the playing field even between the player and trainers.

Note: Vitamins and wings still exist in the world but serve no mechanical purpose. They may be sold for money.
Level Cap Battle Progression

Each gym leader imposes a level cap on the player's team. Pokemon that exceed the cap before the badge is earned will disobey orders in battle. The cap is lifted the moment the badge is obtained. Caps are listed on each area sheet and in the Progression Guide.

Note: Traded Pokemon follow stricter obedience rules as in vanilla. Keep this in mind if you are using trade evolutions.
Progression
Silver Coin System Progression

Silver Coins are part of an informal tradition among dedicated trainers in Johto. They are not rewards for victory — they are markers of personal resolve. Each coin represents a personal goal, a self-imposed challenge, or a turning point in a trainer's journey.

A coin changes hands under two conditions: when a trainer is overcome by someone whose resolve surpasses theirs, or when a trainer achieves the goal they were chasing. In both cases, the coin moves forward because a chapter has ended. The coin does not belong to the strongest trainer — it belongs to the one currently closest to their purpose.

Pokémon Centers: Coins exchanged by the player are returned into circulation — given to new trainers beginning their journeys and used to support future challengers. This keeps the tradition alive without turning coins into relics or trophies.
Progressive Disadvantage Progression Battle

Gym leaders and rival battles scale in difficulty as the game progresses. Early fights are straightforward, but later trainers will have more optimised teams, held items, and stronger movesets — rewarding players who engage with the game's mechanics rather than relying on overlevelling alone.

Sidequests
Legendary and Mythical Distribution Catching Progression

Legendary and Mythical encounters are distributed across three encounter formats so each one has a clear role in progression and routing.

  • Statics: fixed map encounters tied to a specific location and trigger state.
  • Roamers: moving encounters that rotate between routes/maps and must be tracked down.
  • Quests: multi-step sidequest chains that end in a special encounter or reward battle.

Roamer unlock: Roamers are enabled after defeating all 8 Johto Gyms.

  • Legendary Birds: Articuno / Zapdos / Moltres
  • Legendary Beasts: Raikou / Entei / Suicune
  • Lake Guardians: Uxie / Mesprit / Azelf
  • Swords of Justice: Cobalion / Terrakion / Virizion
  • Forces of Nature: Tornadus / Thundurus / Landorus / Eternamours

Dedicated quest chains: some groups are handled as full sidequest arcs instead of roaming encounters.

  • Ultra Beast sidequest: all Ultra Beasts (Nihilego, Buzzwole, Pheromosa, Xurkitree, Celesteela, Kartana, Guzzlord, Poipole, Naganadel, Stakataka, Blacephalon). Unlocks after Kanto Gym 3.
  • Regi sidequest: all Regis (Regirock, Regice, Registeel, Regigigas, Regieleki, Regidrago). Unlocks after Johto Gym 6.
  • Weather Trio quest: Groudon at Mt. Mortar, Kyogre at Whirl Islands (deeper chamber below/behind Lugia's area), and Rayquaza at Mt. Silver summit (highest peak chamber).
  • Eon Twins acquisition: Latias / Latios are received as Steven's gift (ORAS-style).
  • Creation Trio Rift questline: Dialga requires 3 rifts before its encounter, Palkia requires 3 rifts before its encounter, Giratina requires 1 final rift before its encounter, and once Dialga + Palkia + Giratina are all in the player's party, Arceus unlocks at a separate location.
  • Tao Trio questline: Burned Tower for an N encounter + battle (with Reshiram on his team), Seafoam Islands for Kyurem (deep chamber), then Zekrom later at one chosen location: Goldenrod Radio Tower (modern power/ideals vibe), Dragon's Den (dragon shrine vibe), or a custom Ideals Shrine map.
  • Flower Festival questline: National Park sidequest chain: catch multiple Flabebe colors for the Festival Catalog, deliver Scatterbug data for the Vivillon exhibit, enter a Festival Ball-only bug contest where the smallest catch wins a joke reward, then trigger a static Xerneas encounter at the shrine after the contest. Contest entry rule: player may enter with only 2 Pokemon in party.
Rule: Each Legendary/Mythical should be assigned to exactly one of these categories for clean documentation.
Yveltal Questline: Lavender Mourning Rite Catching Progression

A four-step Lavender sidequest themed around mourning rituals, silence, and respect for the life/death cycle before a static Yveltal encounter.

  • Step 1 - Prepare the Rite: show 1 Ghost-type, 1 Normal-type, and 1 Dark-type (or Psychic-type). Reward: Rite Balls x10 (or Dusk Balls) and rite unlock.
  • Step 2 - Quiet Contest (Cry Volume): limited-time, limited-ball event where only Pokemon caught in Rite Balls count. Each catch has a Quietness score (1-5), and highest quietness wins. Entry rule: player may enter with only 2 Pokemon in party.
  • Step 3 - Three Candles, Three Battles: candle battles represent Grief (Ghost-focused), Fear (Dark-focused), and Acceptance (mixed / bulky / status).
  • Step 4 - Yveltal Appears: after the third candle battle, Yveltal appears at the ritual site as a static encounter.

Contest reward options: Mourner's Ribbon (cosmetic), Silencer Charm (temporary lower wild encounter rate at night), or Dusty Bell (flavor key item).

Rule: Quiet Contest results do not change Yveltal availability or stats. The contest reward is flavor/side reward only.
Alola Questline: Star Child, Guardians, and Ultra Beasts Catching Progression

A long-form arc where the player carries a Cosmog Egg, clears four Guardian setpieces with Tapu allies, then unlocks full Ultra Beast capture content.

  • Step 1 - Scientist Gift: the Cosmog Egg is recovered after falling from the sky, then entrusted to the player by a scientist together with the Anomaly Scanner key item. Guardian/UB setpieces only trigger while the egg line is in party.
  • Step 2 - Guardian Setpieces: Viridian Forest, Power Plant, Tohjo Falls, and Saffron City each run as Player + Tapu vs 2 Ultra Beasts. UBs retreat after battle and are not catchable in this phase.
  • Step 3 - Tapu Rewards: each setpiece grants one Tapu as a gift Pokemon (Tapus are not catch battles).
  • Step 4 - Egg Progression: after obtaining 2 Tapus, the Cosmog Egg hatches into Cosmog. After obtaining all 4 Tapus, Cosmog evolves into Cosmoem.
  • Step 5 - UB Capture Unlock: after all 4 Tapu flags are set, scientist upgrades the scanner into Wormhole Stabilizer (or equivalent Solgaleo/Lunala gate), and Ultra Beasts become catchable through controlled quest encounters.
  • Step 6 - UB Finale: Necrozma is the final encounter of the Ultra Beast quest chain.
Rule: Before stabilization, UB setpieces are story encounters only. Catch access starts only after the full Tapu milestone.
Johto Resonance Arc: Rusted Relics and Eternatus Catching Progression

A Johto-centered crisis arc where old tower traditions and modern infrastructure both react to the same growing anomaly.

  • Act 1 - The Towers React: revisiting Ecruteak triggers a Burned Tower pulse and a single autonomous Bell Tower bell ring. Objective is to consult both an elder/monk and a Goldenrod Radio Tower engineer.
  • Act 2 - Two Relics, Two Traditions: Rusted Sword is sealed in Bell Tower behind a short worthiness gauntlet, and Rusted Shield is sealed in Burned Tower behind a charred-switch/brazier puzzle.
  • Act 3 - The Heroes Answer: Sword at Bell Tower courtyard triggers Zacian (static/catchable), and Shield in Burned Tower deep chamber triggers Zamazenta (static/catchable), framed as foreign guardians answering Johto's sacred call.
  • Act 4 - Hidden Core: the anomaly source is below Goldenrod Radio Tower (sealed sublevel / basement chamber) with emergency lockdown progression, corrupted signal rooms, 2-3 battles, and a final Eternatus encounter.
Story tone: Bell Tower carries the holy protector motif, Burned Tower carries disaster-defense ruins, and Goldenrod represents modern amplification where the foreign core finally surfaces.

Ogerpon Questline: The Masked Cave Catching Progression

A mask-gated cave quest that culminates in a multi-phase Ogerpon boss sequence before the catch resolution.

  • Step 1 - Sealed Cave: cave door checks inventory for three masks. If one or more are missing, access is denied with a missing-mask message. Optional visual: each engraving glows if its mask is already owned.
  • Step 2 - Loyal Three Rewards: each mask is obtained after a Loyal Three boss (or mini-quest + boss): Okidogi -> Mask A, Munkidori -> Mask B, Fezandipiti -> Mask C.
  • Step 3 - Four-Stage Ogerpon Battle: scripted gauntlet of Base form, then Mask 1 revive, Mask 2 revive, and Mask 3 revive. Recommended flow includes forced heal windows between phases for pacing control.
  • Step 4 - Catch Resolution: after phase 4, either run a dedicated catch battle as phase 5 or transition directly into a catch state from the final phase.
  • Step 5 - Post-Fight State: cave calms, and mask items become recognized for future form-change use (or remain as story relics if locked).
Balance note: Difficulty can be tuned per phase by changing moveset role/ability profile and by deciding whether phase breaks restore the player's team.
Catching
Apricorn Lure System (Planned) Catching Progression

Apricorns will be craftable into consumable Lures. A Lure does not force one species; it reweights encounter odds toward a chosen lure group. This keeps routing deterministic without making rare targets guaranteed.

Rules lock: one active lure at a time, duration is encounter-based (not step-based), and it works on walking, surfing, and fishing tables. Fishing still respects Old/Good/Super Rod pools before lure weighting is applied.

Locked tuning target: Each targeted species can rise up to ~30% per table, non-target species keep at least 1%, and if multiple targets are present they share boost proportionally. Example target: Abra can move from 1% toward 30% instead of becoming guaranteed.
Colored Eggs / Easter Eggs (Planned) Catching Progression

Breeding and reward eggs will be split into 10 color pools based on the Pokedex body color field. Each egg source can point to one color table instead of a mixed species table.

Design intent: Egg pools stay thematic and routeable while still leaving room for rarity tuning inside each color table.
All Starters Available Catching

All starter Pokemon from every generation can be found in the wild at various points throughout the game. Check the area sheets for locations and encounter rates. Starters are typically found at rare or ultra rare encounter rates.

Quality of Life
Reusable Items Quality of Life Battle

Item economy is tuned around planning, not one-time losses. Berries, TMs, and HMs are all reusable, so key tools stay available for future battles and routes instead of being burned after one use.

Held items are treated as reusable equipment as well. If an item is consumed or triggered in battle, it returns to your inventory after the fight so your core sets remain intact.

Rule summary: Berries, TMs, HMs, and held items are permanent resources in this hack.
Pokemon Center Puzzle Room Quality of Life

A special puzzle room is available in the Pokemon Center. Each puzzle gives you a pre-set team and a scenario to solve — designed to teach battle mechanics through hands-on challenges. Completing a puzzle for the first time awards coins redeemable at the special shop. Puzzles can be replayed freely after clearing them.

Note: Coin rewards are one-time only. Replaying a puzzle after clearing it gives no additional coins.
